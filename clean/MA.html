Resource Information on the Outbreak of Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19)

The Massachusetts Department of Public Health is working closely with the federal Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to provide updated information about the novel coronavirus outbreak.

Binder organization: Department of Public Health
Date published: March 13, 2020

Overview

Recently, a new coronavirus—Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19)—was detected in Wuhan, China. This novel coronavirus causes a respiratory (lung) infection.

View today's current cases in Massachusetts.

For the latest on case counts in the United States, visit the CDC website.

On March 10, Governor Charlie Baker declared a state of emergency, giving the Administration more flexibility to respond to the outbreak. Read more about the declaration.

On March 13, Governor Baker issued an emergency order prohibiting most gatherings of over 250 people in the Commonwealth. Read more about the order.
