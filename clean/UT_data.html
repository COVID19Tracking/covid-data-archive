CoronaVirus (COVID-19) Updates

Latest: New Cases Confirmed, Ski Resorts Closing


Slopes shut down: All of the ski resorts in the Cottonwood Canyons and Park City area have suspended operations for at least a week. Powder Mountain will remain open but limit day passes to 1,000. Eagle Point in southern Utah is closing for the season. Ski Utah is maintaining a list

New cases: Salt Lake County announced nine new cases

The first case of community spread was identified Saturday when a Summit County resident tested positive

Schools, churches canceled: Beginning Monday, March 16, Utah's public schools will cancel all classes and close their buildings to most activities to slow the spread of COVID-19.

Gov. Gary Herbert announcedrecommendations

Many local governments have closed public buildings, including recreation centers, libraries, and senior centers. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints has canceled all group activities worldwide, including sacrament meetings previous decisions

The Utah System of Higher Education, meanwhile, is closing

State officials are encouraging everyone to take serious precautions, especially staying at least six-feet away from other people and staying home
