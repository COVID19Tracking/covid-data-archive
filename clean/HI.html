The Centers for Disease Control (CDC) and Hawai‘i Department of Health have been tracking a contagious respiratory disease caused by a novel (new) coronavirus first detected in China in December 2019. The virus has now been detected in more than 150 locations internationally, including the United States, with confirmed cases in Hawai‘i. The technical name for the virus is “SARSCoV-2”; the disease caused by the virus is called “coronavirus disease 2019,” abbreviated “COVID-19”.
