COVID-19: Situation overview

DHSS is closely monitoring the rapidly-emerging outbreak of COVID-19, a respiratory illness first identified in Wuhan, Hubei Province, China in late December 2019. Cases have been identified in a growing number of locations, including the United States. We are working with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and other federal, Tribal, state and local partners to respond to this evolving public health threat.

There is currently one confirmed case in Alaska. This is a travel-associated case of COVID-19, not a case of community-acquired infection.
Visit the case count page for Alaska, national and global numbers.

New CDC Guidance

  • :
    Community mitigation is a set of actions that persons and communities can take to help slow the spread of respiratory virus infections.
  • People at Risk for Serious Illness from COVID-19:
    Those at higher risk of getting very sick from COVID-19 (older people and those with underlying health conditions) should:
    • 1) Stock up on supplies;
    • 2) Take everyday precautions to wash your hands, keep away from others who are sick and avoid crowds as much as possible. During a COVID-19 outbreak in your community, stay home as much as possible.

    Tips for homes, businesses, schools and more.
